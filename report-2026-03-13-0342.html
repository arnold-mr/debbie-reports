Section 01

Topline Summary

This study surveyed 1,012 U.S. consumers all actively planning a vehicle purchase within the next 12 months. The biggest headline is that the buying process itself is broken in consumers' eyes — over half cite price negotiation and fee opacity as their primary frustrations, and open-ended responses overwhelmingly demand upfront, transparent pricing. Budget skews practical, with 82% spending under $50,000, and flexibility dominates preferences: 40% are open to new or used, and purchase motivations are evenly split across upgrades, life changes, and lease endings. Brands and retailers that lead with radical pricing transparency and streamlined digital tools stand to capture an outsized share of this motivated, budget-conscious buyer pool.

Section 02

Sample Overview

All 1,012 respondents are confirmed in-market vehicle shoppers with a purchase planned within 12 months. The sample skews toward prime earning and family-formation ages, with 62% falling between 25 and 54 years old.

Section 03

Purchase Timing And Motivation Insight

The buyer pool is highly activated: 25% plan to purchase within 3 months and 39% within 3 to 6 months, meaning nearly two-thirds are making a decision imminently. Purchase drivers are evenly distributed — upgrades, life changes, lease endings, and replacement needs each account for roughly 21 to 24% — suggesting no single trigger dominates and broad messaging will be needed.

Section 04

Budget And Financing Insight

The majority of shoppers (82%) are working within a sub-$50,000 budget, with the $25,000 to $34,000 range leading at 26%. Financing through a bank or credit union is the top payment method at 36%, followed by dealership financing at 25%, while 21% plan to pay cash — signaling that financing experience and rate competitiveness are critical conversion levers.

Section 05

Vehicle Preference And Brand Insight

SUVs and crossovers collectively account for 51% of body style interest, with trucks adding another 18%, pointing to a utility-first market. Ford leads brand consideration at 19%, followed closely by Kia (18%), Chevrolet and Hyundai (16% each), while mainstream volume brands dominate and luxury marques trail significantly. Forty percent of buyers remain open to both new and used, creating a prime opportunity for certified pre-owned programs.

Section 06

Feature Priority Insight

Technology and infotainment tops the feature priority list at 41%, narrowly ahead of brand reliability (39%), cargo and passenger space (38%), and safety ratings and fuel efficiency (37% each). The near-uniform spread across the top eight features signals that buyers are holistic evaluators — no single feature wins the sale, and product messaging must address multiple dimensions simultaneously.

Section 07

Powertrain And Ev Insight

Gasoline-only powertrains still command a majority at 50%, but 43% of buyers express interest in an electrified option when hybrid (22%), PHEV (10%), and fully electric (11%) are combined. This signals a market in active transition, and the open-ended mention of EV charging infrastructure as a buying-process gap suggests that information availability — not just product availability — is a barrier to EV conversion.

Section 08

Key Finding 1

Transparency is the single most powerful lever in this market. Price negotiation frustration (29%) and hidden fees (22%) together represent 51% of top frustrations, and the open-ended responses amplify this overwhelmingly — consumers consistently ask for out-the-door pricing online, fixed pricing models, and clear total cost of ownership. Any brand or retailer that removes ambiguity from the price conversation has a direct competitive advantage.

Section 09

Key Finding 2

This is a flexible, value-driven buyer cohort that resists easy segmentation. Forty percent are open to new or used vehicles, purchase motivations are almost perfectly evenly distributed across five drivers, and feature priorities show minimal variance across the top eight attributes. This diversity means one-size-fits-all campaigns will underperform — personalization and configurator-style digital tools that adapt to individual buyer contexts will be essential.

Strategic Recommendations

1

Prioritize a fully transparent, online out-the-door pricing tool that surfaces total cost of ownership — including financing, insurance estimates, and maintenance — to directly address the 51% of buyers frustrated by price opacity.

2

Develop personalized digital shopping experiences with side-by-side trim comparisons and flexible new-versus-used filtering to serve the 40% of undecided buyers and accelerate purchase decisions among the 64% planning to buy within six months.
