03 // Key Findings

PURCHASE DRIVERS & TIMELINE — The study sample reflects a broad and demographically diverse in-market audience, with the highest concentration of active shoppers in the 25–34 age cohort (22%), followed closely by the 35–44 segment (20%). The majority of respondents are shopping within a 3–6 month window, suggesting an active and engaged buyer pool ready to convert with the right offer.

BUDGET & FINANCING — The majority of consumers (58%) are budgeting under $35,000, with the $25K–$34,999 range being the single largest budget tier at 24%. Bank and credit union loans dominate financing at 38%, suggesting consumers are arriving with pre-arranged financing and reduced reliance on dealer F&I departments.

POWERTRAIN TRENDS — Gasoline remains dominant at 45%, but the electrification signal is clear — 45% of shoppers are considering some form of electrified powertrain combined. Fully electric consideration sits at 13%, concentrated among higher-budget and younger shoppers, indicating EV demand is real but still stratified by income and age.

CONSUMER PAIN POINTS — Price negotiation (28%) and fee transparency (22%) together account for 50% of all pain points cited — a systemic trust deficit in the traditional dealership model and a clear opening for brands that lead with transparent, fixed-price purchasing experiences.

05 // Strategic Recommendations
01
Lead with Transparent, Fixed Pricing
With 50% of consumers citing price negotiation and hidden fees as their top frustrations, brands and dealers that adopt fixed, no-haggle pricing models will have a significant competitive advantage in converting this skeptical but engaged buyer pool.
02
Invest in End-to-End Digital Purchase Pathways
Consumers want to complete more of the purchase journey online. Dealers and OEMs that offer digital purchasing — including financing, trade-in valuation, and delivery — will capture the growing segment of buyers who want minimal dealership interaction.
03
Position Electrified Options at Every Budget Tier
With 45% of in-market shoppers open to electrified powertrains, EV and hybrid consideration is no longer niche. Brands should ensure electrified options are visible and comparable at every budget tier — not just premium — to capture the growing mid-market EV-curious buyer.
