U.S. Vehicle Purchase Intent & Car Buying Experience Study

Section 01

Topline Summary

Among 1,012 active vehicle intenders — all confirmed in-market within 12 months — the dominant story is a buyer who is value-conscious, powertrain-flexible, and deeply frustrated with dealership opacity. While gasoline remains the preferred powertrain at 50%, nearly half of respondents are open to electrified options, and 40% are undecided between new and used, signaling a market wide open to influence. Price negotiation and hidden fees collectively frustrate more than half of all buyers, pointing to a clear competitive opening for brands and retailers who lead with transparent, frictionless purchasing. The immediate strategic priority is simplifying the financing and pricing experience — this is the single loudest signal in the data.

Section 02

Sample Overview

All 1,012 respondents are confirmed in-market vehicle buyers planning a purchase within the next 12 months. The sample skews toward adults aged 25-54, who together represent 62% of respondents, with strong representation across all adult age groups.

Section 03

Purchase Timeline And Motivation Insight

Purchase urgency is high: 25% plan to buy within 3 months and 39% within 3-6 months, meaning nearly two-thirds of the sample are actively in-decision. Purchase drivers are evenly distributed — upgrade desire, life changes, lease endings, and first-time purchases each account for roughly 22-24% — indicating no single dominant trigger and a broadly diverse buyer pool.

Section 04

Vehicle Preference And Budget Insight

Forty percent of buyers are open to new or used, with used-only preference (33%) outpacing new-only (27%), reflecting budget sensitivity across the sample. The $25,000-$34,000 range is the most cited budget tier at 26%, and when combined with the $15,000-$24,000 segment (22%), nearly half of buyers are targeting vehicles under $35,000 — reinforcing value as a central purchase driver.

Section 05

Brand And Body Style Insight

Ford leads brand consideration at 19%, followed closely by Kia (18%), Chevrolet (16%), and Hyundai (16%), while Toyota and Honda each sit at 14% — suggesting a competitive and fragmented consideration set with no dominant player. SUVs (30%) and crossovers (21%) together command 51% of body style interest, confirming the continued dominance of utility vehicles over sedans (16%) and trucks (18%).

Section 06

Feature Priority Insight

Technology and infotainment tops the feature priority list at 41%, followed by brand reliability (39%), cargo and passenger space (38%), safety ratings and fuel efficiency (both 37%), and price-value (36%) — a remarkably tight spread indicating buyers are not willing to trade off across dimensions. Electric and hybrid drivetrains also rank competitively at 35%, suggesting electrification is increasingly a feature expectation rather than a niche preference.

Section 07

Dealership Experience Insight

Price negotiation is the top pain point at 29%, and lack of fee transparency adds another 22%, meaning more than half of buyers cite opacity as their primary frustration. Open-ended responses reinforce this strongly, with repeated calls for out-the-door pricing online, fixed pricing models, and simplified financing — the process friction is not incidental but systemic.

Section 08

Key Finding 1

Gasoline remains the majority powertrain preference at 50%, but the electrified segments — hybrid at 22%, EV at 11%, and PHEV at 10% — collectively represent 43% of intent. Combined with the 35% who ranked electric or hybrid drivetrains as a top-three feature, this signals that electrification consideration has crossed into the mainstream and will only accelerate as infrastructure and model availability improve.

Section 09

Key Finding 2

The car buying process itself is a brand differentiator. Across both the closed-ended frustration question and open-ended verbatims, buyers consistently call out the same cluster of issues: hidden fees, high-pressure sales tactics, slow paperwork, and opaque financing. Retailers and OEMs offering transparent digital purchasing pathways are positioned to capture disproportionate share among this frustrated majority.
