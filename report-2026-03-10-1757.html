Section 01

Sample Overview

The respondent pool of 1,012 active vehicle intenders skews toward working-age adults, with the 25-34 cohort leading at 23%, followed by 45-54 at 20% and 35-44 at 19%. All respondents confirmed purchase intent within 12 months, with 25% planning to buy within 3 months, 39% within 3-6 months, and 35% within 6-12 months, indicating a broadly near-term, high-urgency sample.

Section 02

Purchase Timing Insight

A significant 64% of buyers plan to purchase within 6 months, combining the within-3-months (25%) and 3-6-month (39%) segments. This compressed purchase window signals that brand and retail touchpoints must be optimized immediately to capture in-market demand before it converts elsewhere.

Section 03

Purchase Motivation Insight

Purchase drivers are strikingly balanced across the top categories — upgrade desire, life changes, lease endings, first-time purchase, and replacement needs each register between 21% and 24%. This near-equal distribution suggests no single life trigger dominates the market, requiring messaging strategies that speak to multiple motivation profiles simultaneously.

Section 04

Vehicle Preference Insight

The largest single segment — 40% — remains open to both new and used vehicles, while 33% prefer used only and 27% prefer new only. The openness of the plurality creates a significant conquest opportunity for brands and dealers who can present compelling value cases across both inventory types, particularly on financing and trim-level value.

Section 05

Budget Insight

The $25,000-$34,999 and $15,000-$24,999 bands are tied as the top budget ranges at 26% and 22% respectively, while the $35,000-$49,999 band follows closely at 21%. Combined, buyers under $50,000 represent the vast majority of the market, aligning with the strong used-vehicle preference and value-driven feature priorities observed across the survey.

Section 06

Financing Insight

Bank and credit union auto loans are the preferred financing method at 36%, outpacing dealership financing at 25% and cash purchases at 21%. With only 6% undecided on financing method, most buyers arrive with a financing plan in mind, suggesting dealers have limited opportunity to introduce financing late in the process and must engage buyers earlier in the funnel.

Section 07

Brand Consideration Insight

Domestic brands lead consideration with Ford at 19%, Chevrolet at 16%, and GMC and Ram adding further volume, while Korean brands Kia (18%) and Hyundai (16%) are strongly competitive against Japanese stalwarts Toyota (14%) and Honda (14%). Luxury and EV brands remain fringe considerations, with Tesla, BMW, Audi, Mercedes-Benz, and Lexus all registering at 5% or below.

Section 08

Feature Priority Insight

The top eight features are remarkably tightly clustered between 35% and 41%, led by technology/infotainment (41%), brand reliability (39%), cargo/passenger space (38%), and safety ratings (37%). The near-parity across all eight attributes, including electric/hybrid drivetrain at 35%, indicates buyers are multi-dimensional in their priorities and resist single-feature marketing narratives.

Section 09

Powertrain Insight

Gasoline-only powertrains remain the dominant preference at 50%, but the combined interest in hybrid, PHEV, and fully electric options totals 43%. This near-even split between traditional and electrified preferences underscores a market at an inflection point, where EV infrastructure concerns — surfaced in verbatims — likely suppress full EV conversion despite meaningful underlying interest.

Section 10

Body Style Insight

SUVs lead body style preference at 30%, followed by crossovers at 21% and trucks at 18%. Together, SUVs and crossovers capture 51% of intent, confirming the continued dominance of utility-oriented form factors, while the 6% undecided segment remains persuadable through targeted model showcasing.
