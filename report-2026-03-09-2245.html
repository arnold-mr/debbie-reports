Executive Summary

This study reveals a highly engaged but price-sensitive automotive consumer base, with the largest share of respondents (45%) remaining open to both new and used vehicles and clustering within the $25,000–$34,999 budget tier. Gasoline powertrains continue to lead powertrain preference at 45%, though hybrid and electric options collectively command a notable 45% combined share, signaling a meaningful and accelerating shift in consumer orientation toward electrification. The SUV and crossover body styles dominate intent, together representing over half of all body style preferences, reflecting broader market trends toward utility-forward vehicles. Critically, dealership experience remains a significant source of consumer friction—particularly around pricing transparency and fee disclosure—presenting a clear strategic imperative for retailers to modernize and simplify the purchase journey.
