Section 01

Sample Overview

The study reflects a broad cross-section of U.S. automotive consumers spanning all adult age groups. The 25-44 demographic represents the largest combined segment at 42 percent, signaling strong purchase intent among younger and mid-career buyers.

Section 02

New Vs Used Insight

Nearly half of respondents are open to both new and used vehicles, suggesting price and value drive decisions more than vehicle status. Dealers and platforms that present both options fluidly stand to capture the largest share of consideration.

Section 03

Budget Insight

The $15K to $35K range captures 46 percent of respondents, confirming the mid-market as the dominant opportunity. Only 19 percent are targeting vehicles priced above $50K, indicating that premium and luxury segments remain a smaller but notable niche.

Section 04

Financing Insight

Bank loans are the preferred financing method at 38 percent, with dealership financing a secondary choice at 25 percent. The 18 percent cash segment is meaningful and suggests a value-conscious buyer who may resist add-ons and extended terms.

Section 05

Powertrain Insight

Gasoline remains dominant at 45 percent but the combined alternative powertrain interest of 45 percent signals a market in active transition. EV consideration at 13 percent and hybrid at 22 percent reflect growing but not yet mainstream adoption.

Section 06

Body Style Insight

SUVs and crossovers together command 52 percent of preference, confirming the continued dominance of utility-oriented vehicles. Trucks hold a strong 18 percent share, while sedans and minivans continue their long-term structural decline.

Section 07

Pain Points Insight

Price negotiation and fee transparency together account for 50 percent of consumer frustrations, making transactional trust the defining challenge in the purchase experience. Inventory concerns and financing complexity round out the top pain points, highlighting systemic dealership friction.

Section 08

Key Finding 1

Transactional trust is the most urgent consumer need, with 50 percent citing pricing opacity and hidden fees as top frustrations. Brands and dealers who lead with all-in pricing will gain a measurable competitive advantage.

Section 09

Key Finding 2

The mid-market budget range of $15K to $35K is the largest opportunity zone, representing nearly half of all buyers. Products and financing programs designed for this segment will drive the highest volume outcomes.

Section 10

Key Finding 3

Alternative powertrain consideration has reached parity with gasoline preference in aggregate, reflecting a consumer base actively evaluating its options. Dealers who invest in EV and hybrid education and inventory will be better positioned for near-term demand.

Section 12

Strategic Recommendations

1

Implement all-inclusive upfront pricing displays both online and in-showroom to directly address the 50 percent of buyers frustrated by fee opacity and negotiation dynamics.

2
