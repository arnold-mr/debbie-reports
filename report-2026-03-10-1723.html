Section 01

Sample Overview

All 1,012 respondents are confirmed in-market vehicle buyers planning a purchase within the next 12 months. The sample skews toward working-age adults, with the largest cohorts aged 25-34 (23%), 45-54 (20%), and 35-44 (19%). Purchase timelines are distributed across near-term (within 3 months: 25%), mid-term (3-6 months: 39%), and longer-term (6-12 months: 35%) windows.

Section 02

Purchase Intent Insight

The purchase triggers are evenly distributed across upgrade desire, life changes, lease expirations, first-time buying, and replacement needs — each registering between 21% and 24%. This near-uniform spread signals that no single motivational segment dominates, and messaging strategies should be modular rather than built around one core use case.

Section 03

New Vs Used Insight

A plurality of shoppers (40%) remain open to both new and used vehicles, while used-only preference (33%) outpaces new-only (27%). Verbatim responses reveal that value, depreciation avoidance, and trim-for-budget considerations drive used preference, whereas warranty integrity and safety features anchor new vehicle interest — suggesting that trust and total cost of ownership are the key decision levers.

Section 04

Budget Insight

The majority of respondents (82%) report a total budget under $50,000, with the $25,000-$34,999 and $15,000-$24,999 tiers each drawing 26% and 22% respectively. The $35,000-$49,999 range captures 21%, while only 6% are budgeting $75,000 or more, confirming that this is predominantly a value-conscious, mass-market buyer pool.

Section 05

Financing Insight

Third-party financing through a bank or credit union leads at 36%, followed by dealership financing (25%) and cash payment (21%). Only 12% plan to lease, and 6% remain undecided — indicating that the majority have already formed financing preferences before engaging dealerships, which has implications for how dealers should position F&I conversations.

Section 06

Brand Consideration Insight

Domestic and Korean brands dominate the consideration set, with Ford (19%), Kia (18%), Chevrolet (16%), and Hyundai (16%) leading. Together, Ford and Kia alone account for 37% of brand mentions. Premium European and luxury brands (Audi, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Lexus) each register 5% or below, consistent with the predominantly sub-$50,000 budget profile of this sample.

Section 07

Feature Priority Insight

Technology and infotainment tops the feature priority list at 41%, followed closely by brand reliability (39%), cargo and passenger space (38%), safety ratings (37%), and fuel efficiency (37%). Notably, electric and hybrid drivetrain interest registers at 35% despite only 21% selecting hybrid and 11% selecting fully electric as their actual powertrain intent, suggesting aspirational EV interest that has not yet converted to firm purchase commitment.

Section 08

Powertrain Insight

Gasoline-only powertrains retain a commanding majority at 50%, with hybrid options (gas+electric at 22%, PHEV at 10%) collectively capturing 32% of stated intent. Fully electric vehicles account for just 11%, underscoring that EV adoption remains a secondary preference in this buyer pool despite elevated feature-level interest in electrification.

Section 09

Body Style Insight

SUVs (30%) and crossovers (21%) together represent 51% of body style interest, confirming the continued dominance of utility-oriented vehicles. Trucks account for 18%, while sedans hold at 16%. Only 4% are interested in minivans, and 6% remain undecided — leaving limited room for traditional passenger car positioning.
