Section 01

Topline Summary

This study captures 1,012 active vehicle purchase intenders — all planning to buy within 12 months, with 64% purchasing within six months or less. The dominant theme across the data is a buyer base that is motivated, budget-conscious, and deeply frustrated with dealership opacity; 51% flag price negotiation and hidden fees as their top pain points, and open-ended responses reinforce an urgent demand for transparent, digital-first buying experiences. SUVs and crossovers are the body styles of choice, mainstream brands lead consideration, and while gasoline remains the preferred powertrain, 43% express openness to electrified options — a signal of meaningful transition. Brands and dealers that invest in pricing transparency, streamlined financing, and robust digital tools stand to capture an outsized share of this ready-to-buy audience.

Section 02

Sample Overview

Respondents are all confirmed vehicle purchase intenders planning to buy within the next 12 months, skewing toward adults aged 25-54 (62% combined). The sample reflects a broad cross-section of budget ranges, with the largest segment (26%) targeting the $25,000–$34,999 price tier.

Section 03

Purchase Timeline Insight

The purchase funnel is compressed and urgent: 25% plan to buy within three months and 39% within three to six months, meaning nearly two-thirds of this sample are in active decision mode. Brands and dealers have a narrow window to influence consideration before these buyers commit.

Section 04

Budget And Finance Insight

The sweet spot of the market sits between $15,000 and $49,999, capturing the majority of respondents, with 26% targeting $25,000–$34,999 and 22% in the $15,000–$24,999 range. Financing is largely bank- or dealer-driven — 36% prefer a bank or credit union loan and 25% plan to use dealership financing — making rate clarity and financing transparency a critical conversion lever.

Section 05

New Vs Used And Feature Insight

Flexibility defines this buyer cohort: 40% are open to both new and used vehicles, and verbatim responses confirm the decision hinges on financing rates, trim value, and reliability rather than brand loyalty alone. Top vehicle features — technology and infotainment (41%), brand reliability (39%), and cargo space (38%) — suggest buyers want capable, connected vehicles that hold their value.

Section 06

Powertrain And Body Style Insight

Gasoline powertrains retain majority preference at 50%, but the combined electrified segment — hybrid, PHEV, and full EV — reaches 43%, indicating a market in active transition rather than early adoption. SUVs and crossovers together command 51% of body style interest, pointing to continued dominance of utility-focused form factors.

Section 07

Dealership Experience Insight

Frustration with the buying process is widespread and specific: 29% cite price negotiation as the top pain point and 22% flag hidden fees, totaling over half the sample on transparency-related grievances alone. Open-ended responses amplify this — buyers want fixed pricing, online completion, at-home test drives, and plain-language financing, signaling that the dealership model faces structural pressure from consumer expectations.
