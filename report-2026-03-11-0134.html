Section 01

Topline Summary

This study surveyed 1,012 active vehicle intenders — all planning a purchase within 12 months — spanning a broad age range with heaviest representation among 25-to-54-year-olds. The dominant finding is a market defined by value-consciousness and deep distrust of the dealership experience: buyers want transparency in pricing, simplified financing, and less sales pressure. Ford, Kia, Chevrolet, and Hyundai lead unaided brand consideration, reflecting a strong domestic and value-brand skew consistent with the budget concentration below $50,000. Brands and dealers that lead with upfront pricing, digital tools, and frictionless financing stand to convert these buyers fastest.

Section 02

Sample Overview

All 1,012 respondents are confirmed vehicle intenders planning a purchase within the next 12 months, with 25% targeting a timeline under 3 months. The sample skews toward working-age adults, with the 25-to-54 cohort representing 62% of respondents.

Section 03

Purchase Timing And Motivation Insight

Purchase urgency is high — 25% of respondents plan to buy within 3 months and 39% within 3 to 6 months, meaning nearly two-thirds of the sample are in an active decision window. Purchase drivers are evenly distributed across upgrade desire, life changes, lease endings, and first-time buying, each clustering near 22–24%, suggesting no single trigger dominates and messaging must remain broad.

Section 04

New Vs Used And Budget Insight

Flexibility defines this market: 40% are open to both new and used vehicles, and verbatims confirm that financing rates and deal availability will determine the final call. Budget concentration below $35,000 — with 48% of buyers in that range — strongly favors certified pre-owned and entry-level new trims, and dealers with robust CPO inventory are well-positioned.

Section 05

Brand And Body Style Insight

Ford (19%), Kia (18%), Chevrolet and Hyundai (16% each) lead brand consideration, reflecting a value-and-reliability orientation among this cohort. SUVs and crossovers together command 51% of body style interest, with trucks adding another 18%, confirming that light truck segments remain the primary battleground for this buyer pool.

Section 06

Powertrain And Feature Insight

Gasoline remains the plurality choice at 50%, but electrified drivetrains collectively attract 43% of buyers when EV (11%), PHEV (10%), and hybrid (22%) are combined — a signal that the market is approaching a meaningful inflection point. Top vehicle features — technology/infotainment (41%), brand reliability (39%), cargo space (38%), and safety ratings (37%) — are tightly clustered, meaning no single attribute dominates and product positioning must address a bundle of priorities.

Section 07

Dealership Experience Insight

The buying process itself is a barrier to conversion. Nearly 51% of respondents cite price negotiation or hidden fees as their top frustration, and open-ended responses are saturated with calls for fixed pricing, online completion capability, and reduced sales pressure. This friction is a loyalty and acquisition risk that digitally transparent brands and dealers can directly exploit.
