Section 01

Topline Summary

This study captures the purchase intentions, preferences, and pain points of 1,012 active vehicle shoppers, all of whom plan to buy within 12 months. The headline finding is that transparency and simplicity are the defining demands of today's buyer — from upfront pricing to plain-language financing — and brands that remove friction will win. SUVs and crossovers lead body style preferences, budgets cluster in the mid-market, and electrified powertrains are gaining real momentum at 43% combined interest. The strategic imperative is clear: invest in digital tools that deliver honest, comparable, out-the-door pricing and streamline the path to purchase.

Section 02

Sample Overview

All 1,012 respondents are active, in-market vehicle shoppers with a purchase horizon within 12 months. The sample skews toward prime buying-age adults, with the 25–44 cohort representing 42% of respondents combined.

Section 03

Purchase Timeline And Motivation Insight

The market is urgently active: 25% of shoppers plan to purchase within 3 months, and 39% within 3–6 months, meaning nearly two-thirds will transact in the near term. Purchase drivers are evenly distributed across upgrades, life changes, lease endings, and first-time purchases — each registering around 22–24% — indicating no single dominant trigger and a broadly diverse shopper pool.

Section 04

Vehicle Preference And Budget Insight

Flexibility defines today's shopper: 40% are open to new or used vehicles, and only 27% are committed to new, suggesting certified pre-owned and used inventory strategies hold significant upside. With 82% of budgets falling under $50,000 and the $25,000–$34,000 band leading at 26%, the mid-market is the primary battleground for volume.

Section 05

Brand And Feature Priority Insight

Ford (19%), Kia (18%), Chevrolet (16%), and Hyundai (16%) lead brand consideration, reflecting a competitive domestic and value-import landscape. Technology and infotainment tops feature priorities at 41%, closely followed by brand reliability (39%), cargo space (38%), safety ratings (37%), and fuel efficiency (37%) — signaling that buyers expect a well-rounded package, not a single standout attribute.

Section 06

Powertrain And Bodystyle Insight

Gasoline remains the majority preference at 50%, but electrified powertrains — hybrid (22%), EV (11%), and PHEV (10%) — collectively represent 43% of interest, signaling a market approaching an inflection point. SUVs and crossovers together command 51% of body style interest, with trucks adding another 18%, confirming that utility-form vehicles dominate the current cycle.

Section 07

Financing Insight

Auto loans through banks or credit unions lead financing intent at 36%, followed by dealership financing at 25% and cash at 21%. With 14% of buyers citing confusion over financing terms as a top frustration, there is a clear gap between the channel shoppers prefer and their confidence in navigating it.

Section 08

Key Finding 1

Transparency is the defining unmet need across the entire purchase journey. Between 29% frustrated by price negotiation, 22% citing hidden fees, and open-ended responses dominated by calls for out-the-door pricing and simplified financing, buyers are signaling that the traditional dealership model creates friction at every stage. Brands and platforms that offer fixed, published pricing and plain-language finance tools hold a structural competitive advantage.

Section 09

Key Finding 2

The electrified powertrain segment is a sleeping giant. While gasoline holds a slim majority at 50%, the combined 43% interest in hybrid, PHEV, and EV options — paired with EV charging infrastructure appearing organically in open-ended responses — suggests consumer readiness is outpacing infrastructure confidence. Brands that proactively address range anxiety and total cost of ownership in the buying process stand to accelerate consideration.

Strategic Recommendations

1

Launch or prominently promote a fully transparent digital retail tool that surfaces out-the-door pricing, side-by-side trim comparisons, and plain-language financing terms to directly address the friction points cited by over half of respondents.

2

Develop an electrification confidence package — bundling charging infrastructure guidance, total cost of ownership comparisons, and test drive programs — to convert the 43% of electrification-curious shoppers who are stalling due to information gaps rather than lack of interest.
