U.S. Automotive Purchase Study 2026

Prepared by Debbie  ·  March 2026  ·  n=1,000

Sample Overview

The study captures a broad U.S. adult sample spanning all age groups, with the largest concentration in the 25-44 cohort at 42% combined. Respondents reflect a diverse mix of purchase intentions, budget ranges, and financing preferences, providing a robust foundation for strategic insights.

Age Distribution Insight

The 25-34 and 35-44 segments are the dominant buyer groups at 22% and 20% respectively, representing core household formation and family vehicle purchasing life stages. Younger buyers aged 18-24 and older buyers 65 plus each represent 12%, indicating meaningful opportunity at both ends of the age spectrum.

New Vs Used Insight

Flexibility dominates purchase intent, with 45% of respondents open to both new and used vehicles, suggesting price and value are more decisive than newness alone. Dealerships and platforms that surface both inventory types together are well-positioned to capture this majority segment.

Budget Insight

The $25-34.9K range leads at 24%, closely followed by $15-24.9K at 22%, placing the sweet spot of consumer budgets firmly in the $15K-$35K corridor. Premium segments above $50K represent a combined 19%, signaling a meaningful but smaller luxury-oriented audience.

Financing Insight

Bank loans are the preferred financing method at 38%, reflecting consumer desire for pre-arranged, independent financing before entering the dealership. Dealership financing still commands 25%, indicating the F&I office remains a relevant but increasingly competitive touchpoint.

Powertrain Insight

Gasoline powertrains retain a strong plurality at 45%, but electrified options combined including hybrid, PHEV, and EV account for 45% collectively, signaling a market at an inflection point. EV adoption at 13% lags hybrid at 22%, suggesting range anxiety and infrastructure concerns may still be moderating full EV commitment.

Body Style Insight

SUVs and crossovers together command 52% of body style preference, confirming the ongoing dominance of utility-oriented vehicles in the U.S. market. Trucks remain strong at 18%, while traditional sedans continue their structural decline at 14%.

Pain Points Insight

Price negotiation is the leading frustration at 28%, followed closely by fee transparency at 22%, together accounting for half of all reported pain points. This strongly signals that opaque pricing practices are the single greatest barrier to a positive purchase experience. Only 3% report no frustration, confirming that friction in the buying process is nearly universal.

Key Findings
01
Key Finding 1

Pricing opacity is the dominant consumer pain point, with 50% of respondents frustrated by negotiation dynamics and hidden fees, making transparent all-in pricing a critical competitive differentiator.

02
Key Finding 2

The electrification tipping point is approaching, with 45% of buyers considering an electrified powertrain, yet gasoline still leads individually, suggesting the market is in active transition rather than transformation.

03
Key Finding 3

Digital purchase completion is an emerging consumer expectation, as verbatim feedback reflects strong demand for end-to-end online buying capability with reduced in-person pressure.

Strategic Recommendations
Strategic Recommendations

Adopt and prominently market an all-in transparent pricing model that eliminates surprise fees and reduces negotiation friction to directly address the top two consumer pain points.

Develop a seamless omnichannel purchase experience that allows consumers to complete financing, trade-in appraisal, and final purchase steps entirely online to meet growing digital expectations.

Expand and clearly communicate electrified vehicle inventory and education tools targeting the 35-54 core buyer segment to convert hybrid interest into PHEV and EV consideration.
